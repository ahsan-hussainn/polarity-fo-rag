# ADR-0021: Decision-maker evidence standard

- **Date:** 2026-07-19
- **Status:** Accepted

## Context

Bridge Mandate correction #3. Stage 1 proved the *firm* half of records far better than the *person*
half: principal selection was title rules (`gold/build.py::principal_rank`); the benchmark scoring it
was generated by nearly the same rules yet labeled "ground truth" (422/425 rule-generated, 3 manual
overrides — a **proxy-label benchmark**); email grades measured mailboxes, not people. **Observed**
(WS0 audit): the deleted "Element Pointe / Founders" row proves the extractor could emit non-persons;
no shipped record carries dated evidence of current affiliation. The mandate's chain: deliverable ≠
belongs-to-person ≠ currently-employed ≠ has-authority — each link needs its own evidence.

## Decision

Every person the product presents must pass four separately-evidenced tests, stored on the record:
**identity** (this person, this firm, not a namesake), **current affiliation** (dated evidence —
`affiliation_evidence_url` + `observed_at`; an undated team page proves membership *as of its
capture date only*), **authority** (`authority_basis`: `stated` when a source describes investment
control, `title_inferred` when inferred — and the product says which), and **chain coherence**
(person ↔ firm ↔ domain ↔ mailbox connected by evidence). Primary anchor: **ADV Schedule A** (SEC's
named owners/officers — registry evidence, non-circular) + fresh dated site captures. Since Stage 1
emails were pattern-inferred, even A-grades claim only "vendor reported deliverable; mailbox–person
link inferred from name pattern" — never mailbox ownership. Where no person passes, the record
states "no proven decision-maker" plainly and ranks bottom-of-queue per ADR-0019; the best available
name is **not** promoted. `eval.py` and its docs are relabeled from "ground truth" to proxy-label
benchmark.

## Options considered

- **Option A (chosen):** four-test evidence standard anchored on Schedule A + dated captures.
- **Option B:** keep title-rule selection, improve the rules. Rejected: rules on stale titles can't
  prove identity, currency, or authority — the named Stage 1 failure regardless of rule quality.
- **Option C:** LinkedIn-scrape verification of employment. Rejected for the pre-window pass:
  ToS-constrained at scale and unverifiable capture provenance; Schedule A is public, dated, and
  authoritative for ownership/officers. May supplement (not anchor) in Stage 2.
- **Option D:** drop all persons lacking full proof. Rejected: "no proven decision-maker" stated
  plainly is the mandate's own prescribed behavior; deletion hides the gap instead of pricing it.

## Why this over the others

Each of the four tests answers a distinct question a buyer acts on; collapsing them is how Stage 1
shipped "a mailbox that accepts mail" as "a reachable decision-maker." Schedule A is the only
person-level source in our stack that is simultaneously public, dated, and independent of both our
extractor and our labeler.

## Assumptions and risks

Assumes Schedule A covers the operative decision-makers at small FOs (usually true: owners file) —
misses non-owner CIOs, who then carry `title_inferred` honestly. Dated evidence ages: `observed_at`
is a freshness field the Stage 2 monitoring loop must re-check, or affiliation claims silently rot.
Adjudicating ~89 presented contacts is manual-heavy; accepted for 50 records, must be
tooling-assisted before 450.

## What would change this

Schedule A parsing proving unreliable for >~10% of the 50 (forces a second anchor source before the
450); the Stage 2 brief prescribing its own person standard; a measured re-benchmark showing
`title_inferred` authority labels disagree with stated-evidence labels often enough that
title-inference should be dropped from the product entirely.
